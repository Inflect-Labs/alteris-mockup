# Alteris Financial Group — Suggested Improvements & Inspiration

**Goal:** keep the Alteris brand intact (gold + burgundy + cream; DM Serif Display / Sofia Pro / Poppins; "Prosperity, for life.") while lifting the experience using the best of the reference sites. This is a roadmap of *what to change* and *where the inspiration comes from* — not a rebrand.

See [`alteris-design-system.html`](alteris-design-system.html) for the locked brand foundation everything below should respect.

---

## The references at a glance

| Site | What's worth stealing | Use it for |
|------|----------------------|------------|
| [Rathbones](https://www.rathbones.com/en-gb/wealth-management/) | Audience-first top nav — *Who we help / Our Services / About / Insights / Contacts* | Information architecture |
| [Progeny](https://theprogenygroup.com/) | Homepage that's a pleasure to scroll; Insights pulled onto the homepage with author bylines | Homepage flow + Insights |

| [W1M](https://www.w1m.com/) | Clean, minimal, uncluttered | Restraint & spacing (warm it up) |
| [Chief](https://chief.com/chiefx-and-events) | Events + community programming | Alteris Women: Insights / Events / Contacts |

---

## 1. Navigation — adopt Rathbones' audience-first IA

**What Rathbones does well:** the top nav leads with *Who we help* before *Our Services*. It frames the firm around the client's situation, not the firm's product lines.

**Current Alteris nav:** About Our Team / Services / Insights / Contact / Alteris Women — organised around the firm.

**Recommendation**
- Restructure to: **Who We Help · Our Services · About · Insights · Contact** (+ Alteris Women as a distinct, signposted destination).
- **Who We Help** = life-stage / situation entry points that map onto existing services: *Building wealth · Approaching retirement · Receiving an inheritance · Arranging aged care · Business owners*. Each routes to the relevant service.
- Keep **Our Services** as the capability view (Wealth · Accounting · Insurance · Lifestyle · Care) with the existing mega-menu sub-items.
- Keep the gold pill CTA and dark translucent nav bar — that's brand.

**Why:** lets a worried daughter researching aged care, and a 40-something building wealth, each find themselves in one click — without changing the underlying service pages.

---

## 2. Homepage flow — borrow Progeny's "nice to scroll" rhythm

**What Progeny does well:** the homepage unfolds as a calm, paced narrative — alternating full-bleed imagery, statements, and proof — that rewards scrolling rather than front-loading everything.

**Current Alteris homepage** already has strong bones (cinematic hero → statement strip → services grid → feature rows → reviews → live-the-life → contact). Tighten the *rhythm*, don't rebuild.

**Recommendation**
- Maintain a consistent alternating cadence: dark cinematic → light editorial → warm proof → dark CTA, so each section feels like a "breath."
- Add subtle scroll-reveal (fade/translate-up) on section entry — quiet, premium, never bouncy.
- Make the statement strip a genuine *pause* between hero and services (it already exists — give it more vertical air, per the W1M restraint point below).
- Keep motion restrained: the brand is editorial calm, not a startup landing page.

---

## 3. Insights — the biggest opportunity (Progeny)

**What Progeny does best:** key Insights are pulled onto the homepage, each summary carrying the **author** — a photo + name + role. It humanises the firm and showcases expertise without a hard sell.

**Current state:** the Alteris nav links to `#insights`, **but there is no Insights section on the homepage** (and no author treatment). This is the clearest gap.

**Recommendation**
- Add an **Insights** section to the homepage: 3 article cards (image, category eyebrow in gold, DM Serif Display headline, 1-line excerpt) + an **author byline** (advisor photo, name, role) on each — exactly the Progeny touch the brief calls out.
- Tie articles to the team: "by Gary Gleeson, Senior Adviser" turns content into a credibility and lead-gen engine and cross-promotes advisors.
- Style within brand: cream/white cards, gold category labels, burgundy "Read more" links. A component spec already exists in the design system to extend.
- Phase 2 hook: this same card pattern becomes the home for **Client Stories** later (the brief notes those are a phase-2 effort to pull together).

---

## 4. Restraint & spacing — learn from W1M (but keep the warmth)

**What W1M does well:** exceptionally clean and uncluttered. **The caution from the brief:** "the brand is a bit cold."

**Recommendation**
- Borrow W1M's *discipline* — more whitespace, fewer competing elements per viewport, a stricter type scale — **applied to Alteris's warm palette**, never its cool greys.
- Audit dense areas (footer's 7-column grid, hero's layered metallic panels + sparkles + badges) and simplify: let gold, burgundy and cream do the work against generous cream/white space.
- Net effect: the polish of W1M with the warmth of Alteris — the opposite of cold.

---

## 5. Alteris Women — community-first, à la YPO + Chief

**What YPO does well:** the site is *primarily about the community* — belonging, peers, membership — with Insights/Events/Contacts layered on top.
**What Chief does well:** community expressed through **events and programming** (`chiefx-and-events`) — a clear, aspirational events hub.

**Recommendation for the Alteris Women destination**
- Lead with **community and belonging**, not services. Hero around the network of women, in the Alteris brand (gold/burgundy/cream, DM Serif Display titles).
- Add the three layers the brief specifies on top of the community core: **Insights · Events · Contacts.**
- Build a Chief-style **Events** hub: upcoming events as cards (date chip, title, location, RSVP), past events as a recap gallery.
- Add a softer, warmer **join / express interest** flow rather than a standard contact form — this is a membership feel, not a lead capture.
- Keep it visually distinct enough to feel like its own world, but unmistakably Alteris.

---

## 6. Smaller, high-leverage touches

- **Team as a brand asset.** Both Rathbones and Progeny lean on real people. Surface advisors earlier — as Insights authors (§3) and within "Who We Help" (§1).
- **Proof stays first-class.** The Google 5.0 / 126-review carousel is a genuine strength — keep it prominent; it's the kind of earned trust W1M's coldness lacks.
- **One clear primary CTA per section.** Currently several sections compete ("Book an Appointment" / "Our Services" / "Book a Free Consultation"). Standardise the primary action and reduce secondary noise (W1M discipline).
- **Footer simplification.** Seven address columns is a lot of cognitive load up front — consider a condensed "Offices" block that expands, recovering the W1M cleanliness.

---

## Suggested priority order

1. **Add the Insights section with author bylines** (Progeny) — biggest gap, biggest credibility payoff.
2. **Restructure nav to audience-first** (Rathbones) — reframes the whole site around the client.
3. **Build the Alteris Women community + events hub** (YPO + Chief).
4. **Apply W1M restraint** across spacing, footer, and CTA hierarchy to warm-but-declutter.
5. **Polish homepage scroll rhythm** (Progeny) with subtle reveals.

Everything above is additive to the existing brand — no change to colours, type, logo or voice as documented in the design system.
