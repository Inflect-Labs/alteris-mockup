01 — Identity

Logo & mark

The Alteris wordmark is set in a refined high-contrast serif with a signature gold-to-bronze metallic gradient (#c8b06e → #997847). It is the brand's anchor — always give it room to breathe and never recolour it outside the approved gold, white (on dark) or single-colour treatments.

02 — Colour

Palette

A warm, considered palette: metallic gold for prestige, deep burgundy for warmth and trust, near-black for cinematic depth, and cream/warm-white grounds that keep the brand feeling human rather than clinical. Gold and burgundy are the two carriers of brand recognition.

03 — Typography

Type system

A four-role type system. DM Serif Display sets all titles; Sofia Pro Medium carries subtitles; Sofia Pro Light is the body text; and Poppins handles the header and footer (nav, labels, legal). Eyebrows and CTAs are always uppercase with generous letter-spacing. Sofia Pro is a licensed Adobe Fonts family — load it via your Adobe web kit; this guide falls back to a geometric sans where it isn't installed.

06 — Layout

Spacing & rhythm

Generous, editorial whitespace. Sections breathe at 100–120px vertical padding on desktop; horizontal gutters sit at 64px, tightening to 40px (tablet) and 24px (mobile). Layout is a 4-column services grid and 50/50 feature rows.

07 — Principles

Design principles

Warm prestige

Gold + burgundy + cream signal premium advice without feeling cold or corporate. Avoid clinical blues and greys.

Editorial calm

Serif headlines, lots of air, restrained motion. The page should feel like a considered publication, not a sales funnel.

Generational story

Imagery and copy always span generations — building, inheriting, caring. People, not products.

Earned trust

Real Google reviews, real offices, real names. Social proof is a first-class brand element.

08 — Voice & tone

How Alteris speaks

Do

  • Prosperity, for life.
  • One family. Every chapter.
  • Grow your wealth. Preserve your legacy.
  • For the generation building it. For the generation inheriting it.

Avoid

  • Jargon-heavy product names without human framing
  • Aggressive, urgency-driven sales language
  • Cold corporate "solutions" / "leverage" speak
  • Over-promising returns or guarantees
